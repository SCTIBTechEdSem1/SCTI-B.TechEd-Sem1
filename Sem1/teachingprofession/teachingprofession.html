Teachers in classical and modern times Nepal and India

(Classical Period)

Gurukula System
Gurus taught students in close mentorship focusing on scriptures, philosophy, and life skills.

Buddhist Monasteries
Monks taught at centers like Nalanda, emphasizing logic, ethics, and debate.

Medieval Era
Hindi pathashalas and islamic madrasas taught religion, philosophy, and math Teachers were respected community figures.

(Modern Period)

Colonial Era
British Influence introduced formal teacher training and western subject, teachers shifted to structured curricula.

Post - Independence
Emphasis on teacher training to expand literacy and education access in both urban and rural areas.

Contemporary Era
Teachers now balance traditional methods with digital tools, adapting to diverse students needs in the digital age.
