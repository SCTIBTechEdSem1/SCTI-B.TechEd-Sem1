TVET Development Context in Nepal (Source: TRSP 2080 - 2089)
Before 1911 BS:
• Indigenous skills were transferred from one generation to another through informal methods.
• Kathmandu valley was famous in the region during Malla regime and was known for arts, handicrafts, and specific architecture.
• A family or cast-based traditional apprentice system was the oldest form of occupational skill training that was widely used in Nepal.
• There was a special Decree for citizens to learn some kind of skills and be involved in production and business.

RANA Regime (1911-2007 BS).
• During this period, no TVET relevant national policies except for some programs such as ayurvedic, medicine and veterinary training existed.
• Formal technical education was started in 1930 BS, when a technical school in Kumari Chowk, Kathmandu was established.
• In 1942 BS, engineering section was introduced by offering a two-year suboverseer course for SLC graduates.
• In 1945 BS, the school was shifted to Tri-Chandra College and was renamed in 1950 BS as engineering school.
• In 1958 BS, it was again renamed as Nepal Engineering Institute and started offering an Overseer course in civil engineering and in 1971 BS added an electrical engineering course.
• In 1963 BS, Technical Training Institute was established in Thapathali, Kathmandu and started an Overseer course in mechanical, automobile and electrical engineering. After introduction of New Education System Plan in 1972 BS the present Institute of Engineering (IOE) was formed.

Panchayat Era (2017-2046 BS).
• National Education Commission (NEC) formed in 2011 BS initiated the concept of multipurpose school. It was followed by establishment of Butwal Training Institute (widely known as BTI) in Butwal in 2019 BS.
• Establishment of Balaju School of Engineering and Technology (erstwhile Mechanical Training Centre, known as MTC) in 2020 BS. Technical Training Institute was established in Thapathali, Kathmandu offering 3-years’ Diploma course in 2020 BS.
• In 2027 BS, the government introduced a National Education System Plan (NESP) which prioritized promoting vocational education specifically targeting youths. TVET in Nepal became prominent with the establishment of technical schools in Jumla, Jiri, Lahan, Seti and Dhankuta in 2037 BS.
• The Vocational and Skill Development Training Centre was established in 2034 under the Ministry of Labor. The Technical and Vocational Education Committee (TVEC) and the Directorate of Technical and Vocational Education (DTVE) under the MOE were established in 2039 with its objectives to formulate policies, and manage and implement the system.
• Establishment of CTEVT in 2045 BS

MULTIPARTY DEMOCRACY RULE (2046- 2064 BS).
• Establishment of the Training Institute for Technical Instruction (TITI) in 2048 BS under CTEVT as another milestone in TEVT development.
• This period is also noted for transfer of basic and technician level programs such as community medical assistant (CMA), auxiliary nurse midwife (ANM), health assistant (HA), junior technician/ agriculture (JT/JTA) from Tribhuvan University to CTEVT.

FEDERAL DEMOCRATIC REPUBLIC RULE (2064 BS to date).
• In order to start TVET opportunities from Grade 9, the Ministry of Education started a 4-year technical stream in 2070 BS.
• National Vocational Qualifications Framework (NVQF), started preparations under the auspices of CTEVT in 2071 BS, was approved by the Cabinet in 2078 BS

TVET Institution in Nepal:
Currently, CTEVT and CEHRD are the two major government wings that govern TVET programs in the country. A number of federal ministries and Business and Industrial Agencies also run specific short-term training programs.

Formal TVET programs in Nepal
• CTEVT Diploma/Certificate Programme
• CTEVT Pre-Diploma/Technical School Leaving Certificate (TSLC) Programme
• CTEVT Technical Education in Community Schools (TECS) Diploma Level Programme
• CTEVT Technical Education in Community Schools (TECS) TSLC Programme
• Technical stream in general schools (run by CEHRD), MoE
• Apprenticeship: CTEVT ENSSURE Programme (24 months)
• Staff Nurse courses except CTEVT

Formal TVET providers in Nepal
• Government technical schools (under CTEVT)
• General schools running CTEVT TECS programs
• General schools running technical stream of CEHRD (MoE)
• Private technical schools (affiliated to CTEVT)
• Community technical schools (run by community)
• Technical schools running in partnership basis (different modality)
• Institution under different universities and hospitals running staff nurse and other programs

Non-Formal TVET in Nepal
• Non-formal TVET Programmes locally known as short-term term training courses
• Such vocational training courses (generally less than 12 months duration)
• Dominating duration of short term vocational courses are of 3 months (390 Hrs)
• Skill test is not mandatory but in most of the project based courses, it is provisioned

Non-formal TVET providers in Nepal are
• Council for Technical Education and Vocational Training (CTEVT);
• Private Institutions;
• Other Governmental Agencies;
• Technical Institutions of the Universities;
• Community-based Secondary School (Annex Schools); and
• NGO/INGO.
Source: UNESCO-UNEVOC, 2014, World TVET Database, Nepal
